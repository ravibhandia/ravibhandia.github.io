
Bot Detection for Anchain.AI
---

A team of UC Berkeley students (Devesh Agarwal, Ravi Bhandia, Ming Yu Chi, Jennifer Hu, Aanika Shah) partnered with blockchain security company, Anchani.AI, to build a bot detection and classification tool.

Anchain.AI specializes in blockchain ecosystem intelligence, using artificial intelligence and transaction data to monitor platforms and its activity in real time. AnChain’s goal is to offer individuals the platform and the tools to provide better security and understanding of a platform’s users. Due to overwhelming malignant bot activity on networks, CEO and Founder Victor Fang, asked the team to develop a bot detection tool that distinguishes between human and bot-based transactions.

They spent the semester jumping new hurdles, and developing a tool that allows a user to search for usernames and see a heuristic on the chance that they are a bot. In order to deliver this they went through the data science life cycle to clean and explore the data, engineer features, use clustering and label propagation, and finally supervised learning to make predictions about the identity of users. They also developed visualization tools that could encapsulate a user’s transaction activity for the observer.

Informed by only 3 labelled data points provided in the initial, the team generated over twenty features and used 10-fold cross validation and decision trees to deliver extremely accurate predictions on the data. The team also reported having picked up at least five new skills each, and were able to have first-hand experience working with top-tier industry professionals in the blockchain security space.
